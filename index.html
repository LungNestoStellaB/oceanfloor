The Ocean Zones

The ocean is divided into five major zones, each with its own world of life, pressure, and mystery.

☀️ Sunlight Zone

0 — 200m

Where life is abundant and light penetrates. Home to coral reefs, most fish species, and all marine photosynthesis.

🌅 Twilight Zone

200 — 1,000m

Light fades. Bioluminescence begins. The largest animal migration on Earth happens here — every single night.

🌑 Midnight Zone

1,000 — 4,000m

Total darkness. Crushing pressure. Yet life thrives — giant squid, anglerfish, and creatures we haven't named yet.

🏔️ Abyssal Zone

4,000 — 6,000m

The abyssal plains — the largest habitat on Earth. Flat, cold, dark. Home to organisms that feed on marine snow falling from above.

🕳️ Hadal Zone

6,000 — 11,000m

The trenches. Challenger Deep reaches 10,935m. Pressure is 1,000 atmospheres. Fewer humans have been here than have walked on the Moon.

🌋 Hydrothermal Vents

Various depths

Life without sunlight. Entire ecosystems powered by chemical energy from Earth's core. Some scientists believe life itself began here.
